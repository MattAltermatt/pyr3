🔥 pyr3-peek

A browser-only viewer for fractal flames.

Open a .flame XML file in a WebGPU-capable browser and watch the chaos-game iteration render directly on your GPU. Share the URL with anyone — the flame travels inside the link. No install, no JVM, no native app.

pyr3-peek is intentionally narrow: view a flame, share a link, save your own files. The editor, Forge, Explore, and everything creative-workflow-shaped live in pyr3 (the desktop renderer).

The pyr3 family

  • pyr3 The canonical Kotlin/JVM renderer + editor for desktop. Has the sliders, the mutators, the palette mixer, the everything.
  • ESF The Electric Sheep Fold — a corpus of every Electric Sheep flame, archived and re-collated.
  • pyr3-peek (this site) The browser-only viewer companion. WebGPU, TypeScript, Vite. Read-only by design.
  • flam3 The algorithmic ancestor — Scott Draves's original fractal flame renderer, started in 1992. The chaos-game inner loop + variation library every flam3-family renderer inherits.

Render contract

pyr3-peek is a single-precision (f32) WebGPU renderer. Like every f32 flame renderer in the family, its output is similar but not pixel-identical to the canonical f64 reference in flam3 / pyr3 CPU. Where a genome trips f32-divergent behavior, pyr3-peek renders its f32 result and moves on; the canonical f64 anchor stays in pyr3.
